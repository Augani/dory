# Dory 0.3.2 launch film

## Creative idea

**A workspace that assembles around you.**

Dory begins as its familiar fish mark. The mark releases a small “Core” module, and the rest of the Linux workspace flies in as signed, optional pieces. Those pieces lock together into a graphical Linux desktop, then resolve back into the Dory mark. The metaphor makes the 0.3.2 architecture immediately understandable: one small foundation, many deliberate possibilities.

## Voiceover

Meet Dory zero point three point two.

We took a complete local Linux workspace and made it assemble around you.

Start with a smaller Docker Core. Add only what you need: Kubernetes, Linux machines, or full Debian, Ubuntu, and Kali desktops.

Run and test graphical Linux applications beside your containers—with Retina-sharp windows, persistent disks, scoped Mac folders, and snapshots.

Give AI agents dedicated Linux sandbox VMs. No host files are shared by default. Choose explicit mounts, block network access, roll changes back, and let the temporary VM delete itself when the job is done.

Every optional component is signed, size-verified, and removable. Your workload data stays put.

Now add custom local domains and trusted HTTPS.

Dory zero point three point two. Smaller core. Bigger possibilities. Built for Mac. Open source.

## Platform strategy

Master in `1920 × 1080` (16:9 landscape) for X and LinkedIn. Important copy stays inside a 1720 × 900 safe area. The Dory logo is reserved for the opening and closing. The audio is additive: every claim is present visually so the film works on mute.
