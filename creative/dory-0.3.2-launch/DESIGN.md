# Dory 0.3.2 Launch — Visual Identity

## Style Prompt

An energetic, precise product-launch film in Dory's native visual language: a crisp white landscape canvas, deep navy typography, bright blue systems, and amber connection cues. Components behave like physical magnetic modules—separate, purposeful pieces that snap into a coherent local Linux workspace. Motion should feel engineered but friendly. The Dory fish appears as a strong opening and closing signature; the product story owns the middle. The film must work with sound off and feel at home beside Dory's SwiftUI app and website.

## Colors

- `#F7FAFF` — primary white canvas
- `#07111F` — primary typography and deep structure
- `#0D1B3D` — deep structural blue
- `#147FE8` — Dory action blue
- `#3D7BF4` — logo blue / secondary accent
- `#FFB020` — energy and connection accent
- `#EAF1FE` — cool paper / primary foreground
- `#A9BDD3` — secondary foreground
- `#24B47E` — verified / healthy state

## Typography

- `Manrope` — display and UI copy; heavy headlines against light body weights
- `JetBrains Mono` — release labels, component metadata, routes, and numbers

## Motion Language

- Modules arrive from different axes, overshoot by 2–4%, then magnetically settle.
- Primary scene changes use directional pushes; the graphical-desktop reveal uses one decisive zoom-through.
- Ambient motion is slow orbital drift, scanning lines, and soft radial breathing.
- The Dory fish assembles at the beginning and resolves cleanly at the end.
- The logo does not persist through the feature scenes.

## What NOT to Do

- No purple-to-blue gradients, generic neon cyberpunk, or glowing card grids.
- No unsupported “first” or “only” claims.
- No tiny web-style UI copy; every important message must read on a phone.
- No random motion, jump cuts, or decorative effects that compete with the release story.
- No replacement or reinterpretation of the Dory logo.
