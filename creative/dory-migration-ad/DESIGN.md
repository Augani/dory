# Dory Migration Film — Visual Identity

## Style Prompt

A bright, precision-built developer ad on Dory's warm white engineering canvas. One compact "local world" capsule carries images, volume data, networks, ports, and container state through the entire film. It begins constrained by a source runtime, is discovered by a Dory-blue scan, passes a transparent preflight, crosses a transactional copy-and-verify bridge, and lands in Dory while the source remains visibly present. The film should feel calm under pressure: premium, exact, and optimistic rather than dark or cyberpunk. Sendara's proof-led pacing and continuous-object storytelling are references; its black stage is not.

## Colors

- `#F6F9FE` — warm white canvas
- `#FFFFFF` — elevated product surfaces
- `#071525` — primary ink
- `#0D2A4D` — structural navy
- `#147FE8` — Dory action blue
- `#3D7BF4` — exact logo blue
- `#FFAD1F` — scan, handoff, and current energy
- `#E7F1FC` — cool panel surface
- `#52677D` — secondary copy
- `#20B783` — verified state
- `#DDE8F5` — rules and borders

## Typography

- `Manrope` — primary display and explanatory voice; 800 for declarations, 400/600 for support.
- `SF Mono` / system monospace — runtime names, object labels, stages, policies, and proof states.
- Display tracking: `-0.045em`; body tracking: `-0.015em`; technical labels: `0.12em`.

## Recurring Motifs

1. **The local-world capsule** — five nested, labelled cartridges that persist across scenes.
2. **The Dory current** — a blue path with an amber traveling pulse that discovers and carries work forward.
3. **The source anchor** — the source runtime stays visible during import so "Dory never deletes the source" is shown, not merely said.
4. **Proof gates** — Discover, Preflight, Copy, Verify, and Commit are physical checkpoints with restrained success pulses.

## Motion Language

- Primary scene transition: directional push, 0.42–0.48 seconds.
- Accent transition: zoom-through at the preflight and import handoffs, 0.45–0.55 seconds.
- Outro transition: gentle focus pull, 0.55–0.65 seconds.
- Entrances vary between banked cartridge arcs, scan reveals, scale locks, and line draws; no repeated fade-up pattern.
- One ambient action per scene: current drift, scanner sweep, gate pulse, transfer progress, or logo current.
- The local-world capsule always moves with visible mass and a 2–3% magnetic settle.

## Resolution and Delivery

- Composition canvas: `3840 × 2160`, 16:9, time-based, 30.2 seconds.
- High-refresh archive: `3840 × 2160`, native 60 fps.
- LinkedIn/high-refresh master: `1920 × 1080`, native 60 fps, H.264 High + AAC-LC.
- X-safe master: `1920 × 1080`, 30 fps, H.264 High + AAC-LC.
- A 30 fps 4K archive is retained for compatibility and reproducibility.
- Important copy remains inside a `3440 × 1800` safe area.
- Burned-in captions stay inside the bottom safe zone; an English SRT sidecar is also delivered.

## What NOT to Do

- No dark canvas, purple gradient, glassmorphism, neon cyberpunk, or generic particles.
- No competitor logos; Docker Desktop, OrbStack, and Colima appear as factual text labels only.
- No unsupported "one click," "everything," "lossless," "zero downtime," or "source untouched" claims.
- No implication that preflight always passes; capacity, collision, and portability checks remain visible.
- No tiny literal app screenshot. Recreate only the exact migration UI structure at social-readable scale.
- Do not recolor, reshape, or approximate the Dory mark.
- No jump cuts, random motion, infinite repeats, or blank transition frames.
